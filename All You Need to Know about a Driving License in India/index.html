All You Need to Know about a Driving License in India

Broadly speaking, there are four types of driving licenses – learner’s, permanent, commercial and international.
1. Learner’s Driving License
The first of these has a short validity of just six months, while the latter can be used for as many as 20 years. Also, one can apply for a permanent license only after the completion of one month since the issuance of the learner’s license. Also, a learner’s license can be received simply by completing a few formalities at the local RTO but the permanent version isn’t issued right away.
2. Permanent Driving License
So, basically, anyone who wants to drive a motor vehicle on public roads and is wondering how to apply for a driving license needs to first obtain a learner’s license from the local RTO. The same is issued for a period of 6 months, wherein the holder can learn to use a vehicle. Before the expiry of the document, the holder needs to visit the RTO and give an exam. A permanent license is issued if the person is able to pass both theoretical and practical tests.
3. Commercial Driving License
Now, this is a special category of driving license that is issued to those who need to drive commercial vehicles of any of these three categories – Heavy Motor Vehicles, Medium Motor Vehicles and Light Goods Transport Motor Vehicles. The eligibility criteria to obtain a commercial driving license is different from that of the above two categories. However, the process of applying for it is the same as the Permanent Driving License
4. International Driving Permit
As the name suggests, this is a permit that is required for Indian driving license holders wishing to drive in a foreign land. The RTO provides this permit as a certification of one’s eligibility to drive in a foreign land. This permit comes printed in various languages to help authorities make note of the holder’s eligibility to drive in a given country. In order to obtain this permit, one is required to have a valid permanent driving license and submit an application in a local RTO. The international driving permit has a validity of only one year.
Follow the road rules
When the number of vehicles is less on the road, it is quite easy to drive. But when the numbers go high, you need to be careful to follow the rules. Follow the signals at all time. Sometimes your hand signals convey the other drivers about your intentions like slow down, turn and allowing of overtaking.

Eligibility Criteria to Apply For a Driving Licence

Provided below is the criteria that an applicant should fulfil to obtain a driving licence-
  • Should be at least 16 years old for legally using a two-wheeler with an engine smaller than 50cc
  • Should be at least 18 years old for legally using a two-wheeler with an engine bigger than 50cc or for using a four-wheeler
  • Should be 20 years old or more for legally using a commercial vehicle
  • Should be aware of all the traffic rules
  • Should have a learner’s driving licence for a minimum of 30 days and a maximum of 180 days before applying for a permanent DL
