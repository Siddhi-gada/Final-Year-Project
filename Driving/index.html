How to obtain driving License

1Application Process for a Driving License:
To get a driving license in India, an applicant can either apply online or can visit the nearest RTO office. These are the two ways through which one can proceed to get their driving license.
The Online Process:
• The applicant needs to check the official portal of Sarathi and download Form 4.
• As per the given instructions, the form needs to be filled up and submitted.
• The forms need to be printed out, and Part D is to be only filled by the parents or the guardian in case the applicant is a minor.
• The form needs to be uploaded with all other required documents.
• A web application number will be generated for reference to track the application status.
• Once the entire application process is finished, a notification will be sent by SMS.
The Offline Process:
• The applicant can download Form 4 from the Sarathi portal or collect the form from the nearby RTO Office.
• Form 4 should be filled up appropriately and should be submitted along with other required documents.
• The applicant can also schedule a slot in the RTO office for the driving license test.
The applicant needs to attend the driving license test based on the specific date and time allotted to them. After qualifying for the examination, the candidate will receive the driving license on the spot or through a post on their registered address. Despite having such a straightforward process, the percentage of applicants appearing for the driving test is as low as 41%.
Final Words:
A driving license is an essential legal document that gives the permit to a driver to ride lawfully on the roads. It can be easily seen that the Government of India took the initiative to amend the Motors Vehicles Act of 1988. This step led to a 10% fall in the rate of accidents all over the country.
With the public conveyance becoming more stressful to ride on the roads, commuters can quickly get access to a driving license. It grants the freedom to drive the vehicle as per the requirement of the rider. In addition to all the factors, a driving license is a widely accepted form of personal identification proof that can be used in various government offices and agencies.

Eligibility for different driving licenses in India:
The eligibility criteria for a driving license in India are mainly dependent on two factors, the type of vehicle and the type of driving license one has. The details of the eligibility criteria are further furnished below. General eligibility
The applicant needs to have familiarity with the traffic rules of the country. Documents related to age proof and address proof need to be submitted to the authority.
Motorcycle with gear (MCWG)
The applicant needs to be a minimum of 18 years of age and should have sound knowledge regarding the traffic rules. Motorcycle without gear: The applicant needs to be a minimum of 16 years of age and should have the permission of their parent or the guardian. Awareness about traffic rules is also necessary.
Heavy Commercial and Transport vehicles
The applicant needs to have a minimum qualification of 8th standard. The minimum age limit varies between 18-20 years depending upon the rules of the state.
Documents required for a Driving License:
When the applicant is applying for a driving license, these are the documents which are generally required:
1. Medical Certificate
A declaration by an authorised doctor
2. Photograph
Passport-size photographs.
3. Proof of Address
An applicant can pick between any of these documents like Aadhar Card, Voter ID Card, Ration Card, Electricity bill.
4. Proof of Age
An applicant can consider documents like Voter ID, birth certificate, passport, an affidavit as to the age proof.
